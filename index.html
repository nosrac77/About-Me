Something I haven't mentioned quite yet is that I have a profound love for video-games. So, in honor of all the games that have been apart of my life, I've made a list of my absolute top ten favorite video-games of all time in a neat ordered list (which pains me greatly and upsets the gaming gods, but unordered lists weren't an option).



Carson's Top Ten Favorite Video-Games

  1. The Elder Scrolls: Oblivion

    2. This game easily impacted me the most. So many days spent getting completely lost in the realm of Tamriel duplicating items, fighting giant spiders with swords and magic, cave-diving, and of course completing every single sidequest before beating the game. To me this game wasn't just a way to pass the time or even just have fun, it was an entire world I could immerse myself in. It was an experience. Bethesda just keeps pumping out hits!

  2. Halo 3

    3. Halo 3 was my SHIT MAN. This game is easily my favorite first-person-shooter and is actually the game I thought about going pro (MLG) with. My friends and I used to play this one together so it was actually kind of like a social activity for us. Dropping f-bombs, getting headshots, yelling at teens over my headset... what's not to love? I was good at this game. I mean really good. If you'd like to test my merit slack me and let's dance!

  3. Final Fantasy VII

    4. I mean come on, Cloud is the best video-game character ever made. He's iconic. The only Final Fantasy character to have ever made it into a Super Smash Bros! Fun fact, I actually named most of my video-games characters Cloud because of how much I loved this game growing up. I probably would've named all of the pets Cloud too if it weren't for my mom.

  4. Portal

    5. Another game I had to add in just because of how classic it is. There's really no other game like it. It's funny, great gameplay, really inventive and challenging puzzles to solve, and also kinda spooky at times too.

  5. Super Mario World

    6. I hope this pick is pretty self-explanatory. This is a classic video-game. Again, just stating facts here. Gimme that cape and lemme fly!

  6. Zelda: Ocarina of Time / Zelda: Majora's Mask

    7. Now I know for some video-game nerds this has the ability to ruffle a lot of feathers. Putting both of these Zelda games side by side may seem wild and inappropriate as Ocarina of Time is widely considered the classic Zelda game while all of it's following games are considered mere shadows of it's glory. But those masks were amazing, and the fact that link could also do insane parkour jumps was an awesome addition too.

  7. Kingdom Hearts

    8. This was a disney game meant for children, so naturally I fell in love with it. This one is near and dear to my heart (no pun intended).

  8. Mega-Man X4

    9. This was a classic for me too. Mega-Man and Zero just looked so cool, and the fact that they had futuristic suits of armor and space weapons was awesome too. One of the first toys I ever owned was a Mega-Man action-figure.

  9. Bioshock

    10. This game is overall one of the best ever made. The cinematography, the musical score, the masterfully crafted cut-scenes, the insane attention to detail, as well as phenomenal gameplay and an amazing story-line, all make this game a classic.

  10. Super Mario 64

    11. This one changed the game! I don't have my statistics in front of me but I'd be willing to bet this was one of the first 3D games ever created, so that's pretty neat. And I feel like the music in pretty much every Mario game is totally underrated.
